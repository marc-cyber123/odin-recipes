Sinigang Na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy is a classic Filipino comfort dish known for its savory and tangy flavor. The sourness, usually from tamarind (sampalok), blends perfectly with tender pork and assorted vegetables, creating a warm and hearty soup thatâ€™s best enjoyed with steamed rice.

  • 1 kg pork belly (liempo) or pork ribs, cut into serving piece
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack sinigang mix (or 1 cup fresh tamarind juice if available)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 pcs long green chili (siling haba)
  • 1 medium radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach)
  • 10 pcs string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1 medium eggplant (talong), sliced
  • 1 medium taro (gabi), peeled and quartered (optional, for thicker soup)
  • Fish sauce (patis), to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

ðŸ”ª Instructions:

  1. Boil the pork:
    • In a large pot, combine pork, onion, and tomatoes. Add water and bring to a boil. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface.
  2. Simmer until tender:
    • Lower the heat and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork is tender.
  3. Add gabi (if using):
    • Add the taro and cook until soft. This will slightly thicken the soup.
  4. Add vegetables:
    • Add radish, eggplant, and string beans. Continue cooking until vegetables are tender.
  5. Season and sour:
    • Pour in the sinigang mix (or tamarind juice) and stir well. Add green chili, fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  6. Finish with greens:
    • Add kangkong last, cook for 1â€“2 minutes, then turn off the heat.
  7. Serve hot:
    • Best served with steamed rice and a side of fish sauce with chili.
